  <p>The step-by-step solution can be explored in the interactive exercise above. The key takeaways are:</p>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Potentiometric contours in the lower sand unit</strong> are evenly spaced vertical lines based on the linear interpolation of head values between the wells, decreasing from left to right.</li>
    <li><strong>The potentiometric surface</strong> is represented by a planar surface (a straight line in cross-section) connecting the head elevations of the two wells.</li>
    <li><strong>The direction of groundwater flow through the upper clay unit</strong> is mainly downward (recharge). This is determined by comparing the water table elevation to the potentiometric surface elevation. Since the water table is higher, there is a downward vertical gradient.</li>
    <li><strong>The expected vertical hydraulic head profile</strong> near Well 2 shows a relatively steep downward gradient through the clay, followed by a relatively constant head (or a much slighter gradient) through the highly conductive sand.</li>
  </ul>
