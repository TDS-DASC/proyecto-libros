  <h3 class="book-title text-[var(--book-title-1)] text-3xl mb-4 mt-6">Problem 6 Solution</h3>

  <p>The key takeaways from this exercise are:</p>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>The water table</strong> in an unconfined aquifer is a subdued replica of the topography, sloping toward the discharge point (the river in this case).</li>
    <li><strong>Equipotential contours</strong> are lines of equal hydraulic head. In the interactive exercise, they are drawn by selecting pairs of points at the same elevation.</li>
    <li><strong>Flow lines</strong> are perpendicular to equipotential lines and indicate the direction of groundwater flow — from higher hydraulic head to lower hydraulic head.</li>
    <li><strong>In an unconfined aquifer near a river</strong>, groundwater flows toward the river, which acts as a discharge boundary. The equipotential lines curve as they approach the river, reflecting the geometry of the flow system.</li>
    <li><strong>The hydraulic head</strong> decreases along a flow line from the recharge area toward the discharge point. The interactive exercise lets you measure this difference (Δh) between selected points.</li>
  </ul>
