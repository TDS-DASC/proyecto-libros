  <p>The step-by-step solution can be explored in the interactive exercise above. The key takeaways are:</p>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Potentiometric contours (Part a)</strong> in the lower sand unit are evenly spaced vertical lines based on linear interpolation of head values between the wells — head increases from left (36 m at Well 1) to right (40 m at Well 2), so contours are labeled 36, 37, 38, 39, 40 m from left to right.</li>
    <li><strong>The potentiometric surface (Part b)</strong> is a planar surface (a straight line in cross-section) connecting the head elevations of the two wells screened in the lower confined aquifer (36 m at Well 1, 40 m at Well 2).</li>
    <li><strong>The flow direction through the upper clay unit (Part c)</strong> is <em>upward</em>. The potentiometric head in the lower sand at Well 2 is 40 m, which is higher than the water table at that location (~38 m), creating an upward vertical gradient through the clay.</li>
    <li><strong>The expected vertical head profile (Part d)</strong> near Well 2 shows a slight downward decrease in head through the unconfined aquifer (due to non-horizontal flow), then a sharp upward jump across the aquitard, then a relatively constant head in the lower confined aquifer.</li>
  </ul>
